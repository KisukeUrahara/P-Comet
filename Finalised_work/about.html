What is P-Comet?

P-Comet is a new and upcoming social media platform which facilitates project collaboration between students from various colleges and/or streams. If you have a knack for inventing, or if you are a crazy coder looking to put your skills to good use on some fancy app, or if you just want to get in on the next big thing, then P-Comet is the place for you.

P-Comet gives you a platform to build and maintain your resume and keep in touch with your team members outside the workplace. Build up your portfolio by working on, or starting projects.

P-Comet is fast, responsive, and user-friendly. Built using Metro UI CSS Bootstrap which gives the website its professional touch, we guarantee your experience on our website will be a pleasant one.

FAQs

1. How does P-Comet differ from other project platforms?

P-Comet is the first website to implement the concept of project recruitment, where the creator of a project may invite others to be a part the project development team. A person may also send a request to join a project. In this manner, a project admin can put together the right workforce he needs to get the job done.

2. How does P-Comet benefit me?

Your profile on P-Comet is like your face to the technical world, and may even be used as a resume or portfolio. You may also invite your seniors or mentors to view your project and assist you through the debugging process so you don't fall short during the exams.

3. What is a project on P-Comet?

A project on P-Comet is just like a group on facebook. Although you may actually be doing your work offline, P-Comet gives you the opportunity to keep in touch with your team mates outside the work place and enables all members to know exactly what each person is doing. You can maintain your project page, post media and code via our thread system which is built into each project page, and collaborate with your team mates as you go about your work.

4. What is the guarantee that my idea won't be lifted?

We have done our best to ensure that your project ideas remain safe through the use of elaborate privacy settings. if you do not want your project to be seen by outsiders, all you need to do is change your privacy settings (which are found on the concerned project page itself).
